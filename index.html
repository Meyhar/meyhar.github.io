Research Experience

Here are some of the key research projects I've been a part of.

GSI Helmholtz Centre for Heavy Ion Research, Germany

Project: Simulation Studies on the UniCell Setup (Summer 2025)
Advisor: Dr. Jochen C. Ballof
I ran SIMION simulation studies on the UniCell, a novel gas stopping cell designed to reduce the extraction time of superheavy elements. When the ion funnel had shorted electrode plates, I developed and validated a method through simulation to restore high efficiency without requiring mechanical repair to the funnel.

Karmanos Cancer Center, Wayne State University

Project: Compton Camera for Medical Physics Applications (Oct 2024 - Feb 2025)
Advisor: Dr. Ramesh Boggula
I researched the application of Compton cameras for imaging gamma rays from patients administered nuclear medicine like Pluvicto (Lu-177). My work and findings directly led to the cancer center acquiring a Compton camera from M3D imaging.

Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB), MSU

Projects: EDM³ and Single Atom Microscope (SAM) (Oct 2022 - Dec 2024)
PI: Dr. Jaideep T Singh
As a student research assistant, I assembled an ion transport instrument using vacuum chambers and ion funnels for the EDM³ project, learning vacuum operation skills. I also built low-noise electrical breakout boxes, reducing noise in pico-amp current measurements. For the SAM project, I constructed a blackout enclosure which reduced optical background photon counts and performed theoretical calculations and analysis using Zemax OpticStudio and Python.

Lyman Briggs College, MSU

Project: Diophantine Triples (Sept 2022 - Present)
Advisor: Dr. Aklilu Zeleke
Under the guidance of Prof. Zeleke, I am conducting number theory research. I created a Python program to generate and filter arrays that fit the conditions of a Diophantine triple, found multiple recurrence relations, and am now in the process of publishing a paper on our findings.

Travel Blog

I have a passion for travel. In the last 3 years, I have been to 39 US states and 9 different countries.

  • United States of America
    • In the US, my travel has been primarily road trips. Especially with one of my best friends from California to Michigan. I have gone to east coast multiple times to visit friends and see NYC, Washington and Boston.
    • My favourite places are the national parks in the US. Especially, crater lake national park in Oregon and Grand Canyon in Arizona. However, the most scenic beauty that I have witnessed by driving was in Utah.
    • I love Michigan as I have lived here for more than 3 years now. It is a beautiful state with great state and national parks. I like to claim that if someone says they can predict michigan weather with 100% accuracy, then either they are lying or they are a god. Michigan weather is one of the most unpredictable things ever. I have witnessed snow, hail, rain and sunny on the same day here. It is kinda fun because you don't know what weather you'll be faced with.
    • My favourite cities in the US include NYC, Washington, Seattle and Chicago.
  • Canada
    • I am yet to travel extensively in Canada. Till now I have only been to Toronto and Windsor. I must say that it is cheaper in Canada than states.
    • Food in Canada is suprisingly good. I love their poutine fries. Also, the Indian food in Canada is quite authentic.
    • Ontario roads are way smoother than Michigan. Sorry MI, gotta be honest.
  • India
    • Since, I am an Indian. You would expect me to have travelled India extensively. However, my travels in India have not been as extensive as the US because I was a kid for the most part when I lived in India.
    • Allmost all of my family lives in India, so I try to visit at least once a year.
    • The food in India is great, though you must be careful if you are trying the street food cause some of that can be injurious to health.
    • I have visited some places in India like Mumbai, Bangalore, Tirupati, Amritsar, Lucknow, Kanpur and Delhi.
    • I think my favourite city would be Mumbai.
  • Thailand
    • This was a family trip to Bangkok and Pattaya for exploring Thai culture and also to visit a friend of my dad's.
    • I think it is a very interesting place with a great culture.
  • Vietnam
    • This was my solo trip to explore the history of Vietnam and explore the vietnamese cuisine.
    • I went to Saigon and stayed downtown. One thing that surprised me was the good Indian food in Saigon.
  • United Kingdom
    • I was in London only for a day. I saw some landmarks like the Big Ben, Hyde park, and Oxford Street.
  • Germany
    • I went to Germany to attend a Summer School at GSI, Darmstadt.
    • This was a great learning and fun experience. I learnt a lot about large scale experiments.
    • Also, made great friends and had a lot of fun with them. Especially the Greeks, the Poles and the Italians.

  • France
    • Went to Strasbourg, what a fantastic place
    • You could see the differences and the influence of German culture on the French one.

  • Switzerland
    • This is perhaps the most expensive place I have ever visited.
    • We went to Basel and for lunch had pizza for $25.
    • Everything in Switzerland, from chocolates to food to travel is expensive. I have been told that Swiss people are well paid so to them it is normal but for anyone visiting from outside it is crazy expensive.
    • I must say though that it is very beautiful and an interesting place.
