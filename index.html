Research Experience

Here are some of the key research projects I've been a part of.

GSI Helmholtz Centre for Heavy Ion Research, Germany

Project: Simulation Studies on the UniCell Setup (Summer 2025)
Advisor: Dr. Jochen C. Ballof
I ran SIMION simulation studies on the UniCell, a novel gas stopping cell designed to reduce the extraction time of superheavy elements. When the ion funnel had shorted electrode plates, I developed and validated a method through simulation to restore high efficiency without requiring mechanical repair to the funnel.

Karmanos Cancer Center, Wayne State University

Project: Compton Camera for Medical Physics Applications (Oct 2024 - Feb 2025)
Advisor: Dr. Ramesh Boggula
I researched the application of Compton cameras for imaging gamma rays from patients administered nuclear medicine like Pluvicto (Lu-177). My work and findings directly led to the cancer center acquiring a Compton camera from M3D imaging.

Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB), MSU

Projects: EDM³ and Single Atom Microscope (SAM) (Oct 2022 - Dec 2024)
PI: Dr. Jaideep T Singh
As a student research assistant, I assembled an ion transport instrument using vacuum chambers and ion funnels for the EDM³ project, learning vacuum operation skills. I also built low-noise electrical breakout boxes, reducing noise in pico-amp current measurements. For the SAM project, I constructed a blackout enclosure which reduced optical background photon counts and performed theoretical calculations and analysis using Zemax OpticStudio and Python.

Lyman Briggs College, MSU

Project: Diophantine Triples (Sept 2022 - Present)
Advisor: Dr. Aklilu Zeleke
Under the guidance of Prof. Zeleke, I am conducting number theory research. I created a Python program to generate and filter arrays that fit the conditions of a Diophantine triple, found multiple recurrence relations, and am now in the process of publishing a paper on our findings.
